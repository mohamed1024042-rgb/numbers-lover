Mathematical Constants

The numbers shaping our geometry, patterns, and calculus

Pi (π) 3.14159

The ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter, fundamental in trigonometry.

Euler's Number (e) 2.71828

The base of natural logarithms, describing compounding growth and exponential decay.

Golden Ratio (φ) 1.61803

Found when the ratio of two quantities is the same as the ratio of their sum to the larger of the two.

Pythagoras' Constant (√2) 1.41421

The length of the hypotenuse of a right isosceles triangle with unit-length legs.

Euler-Mascheroni (γ) 0.57721

The limiting difference between the harmonic series and the natural logarithm.

Apéry's Constant (ζ(3)) 1.20205

The value of the Riemann zeta function at 3, appearing in quantum electrodynamics.

Physics Constants

The unchanging variables that govern the rules of our physical universe

Speed of Light (c) 299,792,458 m/s

The absolute speed limit of the universe in a vacuum, crucial for relativity.

Gravitational Constant (G) 6.674e-11

Determines the strength of gravitational attraction between objects in Newtonian physics.

Planck's Constant (h) 6.626e-34 J·s

The physical constant that relates a photon's energy to its frequency in quantum mechanics.

Boltzmann Constant (k) 1.380e-23 J/K

Relates the average relative kinetic energy of particles in a gas with thermodynamic temperature.

Elementary Charge (e) 1.602e-19 C

The electrical charge carried by a single proton, or the negative equivalent of an electron.

Reduced Planck (ħ) 1.054e-34 J·s

Also known as the Dirac constant, used primarily in quantum orbital momentum calculation.

Chemistry Constants

The values used to compute molecular ratios, reactions, and thermodynamic properties

Avogadro's Number (N_A) 6.022e23 mol⁻¹

The number of constituent particles (usually atoms or molecules) in one mole of a substance.

Ideal Gas Constant (R) 8.314 J/(mol·K)

The constant of proportionality in the ideal gas law equation linking pressure and volume.

Faraday Constant (F) 96,485 C/mol

The electric charge per mole of electrons, used in calculations of electrochemistry.

Rydberg Constant (R_∞) 1.097e7 m⁻¹

Relates to the electromagnetic atomic emission spectrum of elements, primarily hydrogen.

Molar Volume (V_m) 22.4 L/mol

The average volume occupied by one mole of an ideal gas at STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure).

Triple Point of Water 273.16 K

The exact temperature at which solid, liquid, and gaseous water coexist in thermodynamic equilibrium.

Biology Constants & Scales

The key scale dimensions and rates of biological life cycles and mechanisms

Human Body Temp 37 °C

The average optimal temperature required to sustain metabolic reactions within human cells.

Base Pair Rise 0.34 nm

The structural physical distance between two adjacent nucleotide base pairs along a DNA helix.

DNA Width 2.0 nm

The exact outer diameter of double-stranded B-DNA structure widely accepted in biology.

Physiological pH 7.40

The strictly regulated pH concentration level of healthy human arterial blood plasma.

Ribosome Speed 20 aa/sec

The rate at which standard bacterial ribosomes translate messenger RNA into polypeptide chains.

Resting Potential -70 mV

The electrical tension relative potential across nerve membranes at resting state.
